At the Walt Disney World Resort in Bay Lake, Florida, close to Orlando, there is a zoological theme park called Disney's Animal Kingdom Theme Park. The Walt Disney Company, through its Parks, Experiences, and Products division, owns and runs the 580-acre park, which is the biggest theme park in the world (230 ha). The fourth theme park to be constructed in the resort, the park debuted on April 22, 1998, Earth Day. The park is devoted to and oriented on animal and environmental conservation, an idea first put forth by Walt Disney.

At Disney's Animal Kingdom park, one of the biggest animal theme parks in the world, you may encounter exotic animals and thrilling thrills. Walt Disney's commitment to animal care, education, and research is reflected in the park, which is home to more than 2,000 animals from 300 different species. Oasis, Discovery Island, Pandora - The World of Avatar, Africa, Rafiki's Planet Watch, Asia, and DinoLand, U.S.A. are just a few of the fascinating places you can visit.
